A curated selection.

Featured Homes is not intended as a complete public inventory.

Instead, it offers a selective view of the kinds of homes LUX works with - from country estates and restored village properties to coastal residences and more discreet off-market opportunities.

The purpose is to give buyers a clearer sense of quality, setting and style.

[IMAGE: property exterior / editorial architecture]

Examples of featured home types.

Country estate

Restored or historic homes in quieter countryside settings with strong sense of place.

[IMAGE: countryside estate]

Village residence

Properties in historic towns and villages, with architectural character and everyday proximity.

[IMAGE: village residence]

Coastal home

Selected apartments or houses near the Adriatic coast and local daily life.

[IMAGE: coastal home]

What defines a featured home.

A featured home is selected not only for its visual appeal, but for its location, atmosphere and overall fit within the region.

Some homes stand out for architecture.

Others for setting, restoration potential or the quality of daily life they offer.

What connects them is character and long-term relevance.
